the backbone of almost every deep netHe et al. · 2015 Paper 3AlexNet — a deep net that learned its own features from a million images, won 2012 by a landslide and ignited the deep learning revolutionKrizhevsky et al. · 2012 Paper 4Word2Vec — a machine that teaches itself word vectors from massive text; one written sentence is one new recognizer; the foundation of multimodal AIRadford et al. · 2021 Paper 7LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) — a memory conveyor with its weight fixed at 1 plus two learned gates cured vanishing gradients, let AI remember across a thousand steps, and ruled sequence modeling for twenty yearsHochreiter & Schmidhuber · 1997 Paper 8Seq2Seq (Sequence to Sequence Learning) — two LSTMs in relay, "first understand, then re-say": compress a sentence into one vector and generate word by word; neural translation's first win over statistical systems, and the birth of the encoder-decoder paradigmSutskever, Vinyals & Le · 2014 Paper 9Bahdanau Attention (Jointly Learning to Align and Translate) — tear down the "one vector for the whole sentence" bottleneck: let the decoder glance back at the source for every word it writes, allocating its gaze by relevance; attention's birth certificate, grown into the Transformer three years laterBahdanau, Cho & Bengio · 2014 Paper 10BERT — swap "guess the next word" for "fill in the blank" and a model can finally read unlabeled text both ways, teaching itself language; one foundation + one thin head per task broke 11 records, and "pre-train + fine-tune" has ruled NLP ever sinceDevlin et al. · 2018 Paper 11GPT-3 — scale "guess the next word" to 175 billion parameters and the model picks up new tasks on the spot from a few examples, no more training: size buys generality; the start of the prompting era and ChatGPT's direct ancestorBrown et al. · 2020 Paper 12Scaling Laws — a model's skill falls along a smooth power law in "model size + data + compute," straight across seven orders of magnitude, letting you calculate a bigger model's score in advance; the network's wiring barely matters. It turned big models from alchemy into engineering — the quantitative basis for GPT-3 and the "bigger is better" eraKaplan et al. · 2020 Paper 13Emergent Abilities — some skills small models entirely lack appear "suddenly" once a model crosses a scale threshold, like water hitting its boiling point, and can't be predicted in advance: it chisels a "phase-transition" crack into the smooth scaling-law curve, and even chain-of-thought and instruction following only kick in on large models. It shaped how the field talks about large models — and drew the famous 2023 "is it a mirage?" rebuttalWei et al. · 2022
